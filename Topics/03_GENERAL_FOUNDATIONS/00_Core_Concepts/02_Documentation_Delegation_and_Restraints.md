# General Foundations 02: Documentation, Delegation, and Restraints

Date: March 20, 2026

## Documentation Standards Reinforced
Strong charting includes:
- Time stamps
- Objective pre/post data
- Intervention specifics
- Patient response
- Escalation actions and resulting orders

Weak charting to avoid:
- "Tolerated well"
- "Looks better"
- Vague summaries without measurable outcomes

## Delegation Rules Reinforced
Appropriate for UAP:
- Routine, stable, predictable tasks after RN assessment
- Example: ambulating a stable post-op patient

Not delegable by RN:
- Initial assessment
- Teaching
- Evaluation of response to treatment
- Unstable clinical judgment tasks

## Restraint Principles Reinforced
- Use least restrictive interventions first.
- Restraints are last resort for safety.
- If used: frequent monitoring, release per protocol, circulation/skin/needs checks, discontinue as soon as safe.

## Additional High-Yield Point
- Alert adult with no contraindications: oral temperature route is preferred initial method.
