## High-Yield Rules for Quick Reference

### Hand Hygiene
- ✅ Required BEFORE and AFTER room entry (not just patient contact)
- ✅ Soap and water for C. difficile (spores resist alcohol)
- ✅ Alcohol-based sanitizer acceptable for other organisms if hands not visibly soiled

### Transmission-Based Precautions
- ✅ **Suspected = implement immediately** (don't wait for confirmation)
- ✅ TB = airborne (N95, negative pressure)
- ✅ MRSA = contact (gloves, gown; not respiratory)
- ✅ C. difficile = contact + soap/water hand hygiene

### Sterile Field
- ✅ Outer wrapper is NON-STERILE by design; touching it is safe
- ✅ Actual field/contents/drape borders touched = discard and restart
- ✅ Set up field FIRST, hand hygiene, don sterile gloves, THEN remove old dressing

### PPE Doffing (Critical Sequence)
- ✅ Gloves → Gown → Mask → Eye protection
- ✅ Removes most contaminated layers first while gown still protects
- ✅ Each layer removed away from body to contain contamination

### Waste Segregation
- ✅ Regular trash: respiratory secretions, non-bloody items
- ✅ Biohazard red bag: ANY blood contamination, sharps
- ✅ Respiratory secretions alone ≠ biohazard (efficient resource management)

### Occupational Exposure
- ✅ Needlestick: Report to occupational health IMMEDIATELY (PEP window 2 hours)
- ✅ Assume positive risk; don't wait for patient test results for PEP decision
- ✅ Sequence: Report → baseline labs → PEP assessment → wound care → documentation

### Patient Sequencing
- ✅ Visit in order: Clean → Contact → Droplet → Airborne
- ✅ Prevents bringing high pathogens to unprotected patients
