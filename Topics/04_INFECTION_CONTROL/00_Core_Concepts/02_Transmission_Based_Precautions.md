# Transmission-Based Precautions: Airborne, Droplet, Contact

## Core Concept: Implement Based on Suspected Diagnosis, Not Confirmed Status

**Golden Rule:** Act on suspicion, not confirmation. If clinical presentation suggests infection, implement precautions immediately while awaiting culture results.

---

## 1. AIRBORNE PRECAUTIONS

### Pathogens Requiring Airborne Precautions
- **Tuberculosis (TB)** – pulmonary or laryngeal
- **Measles** (rubeola)
- **Varicella** (chickenpox, disseminated zoster)
- SARS-CoV-2 (during certain phase if aerosolized risk)

### Implementation
- **N95 respiratory mask** (not surgical mask) for all HCW room entry
- **Negative-pressure room** (if available/required per facility)
- Door kept closed
- Patients wear surgical mask when leaving room
- Duration: Until cleared by MD (typically 24 hours post-antibiotic initiation for TB)

### Key HESI Principle
- **Suspected TB** = airborne precautions immediately
- Do NOT wait for sputum culture confirmation—delay increases HCW exposure risk
- TB risk is respiratory droplet-sized particle nuclei, can remain suspended for hours

---

## 2. DROPLET PRECAUTIONS

### Pathogens Requiring Droplet Precautions
- Influenza (flu)
- Pertussis (whooping cough)
- Meningococcal meningitis
- Mumps
- Rubella
- Some coronavirus strains (non-airborne phase)

### Implementation
- **Surgical mask** (not N95) for HCW within 3-6 feet of patient
- Eye protection if splashing risk
- Patient wears mask when leaving room
- Door may remain open
- Gloves and gown if contact with body fluids likely

### Key Distinction from Airborne
- Droplets do NOT remain suspended for prolonged periods
- Clear physical barrier (mask) sufficient; negative pressure not required
- Larger droplet particles, shorter transmission distance

---

## 3. CONTACT PRECAUTIONS

### Pathogens Requiring Contact Precautions
- **MRSA** (methicillin-resistant *Staphylococcus aureus*)
- *Clostridioides difficile*
- Vancomycin-resistant Enterococcus (VRE)
- Norovirus (highly contagious, staff with acute GI symptoms should not work)
- Extended-spectrum beta-lactamase (ESBL) producers
- Multidrug-resistant gram-negatives

### Implementation
- **Gloves** required for patient contact and potentially contaminated surfaces
- **Gown** if contact with skin or contaminated surfaces expected
- dedicated equipment (thermometer, BP cuff, stethoscope) if possible
- Hand hygiene with soap and water preferred (especially if spore-forming pathogens)
- Single-patient rooms preferred; shared rooms with same organism acceptable

### Key Principle for MRSA
- **Suspected MRSA = contact precautions immediately**
- MRSA spreads via skin-to-skin and contaminated surfaces, NOT respiratory
- Gloves and standard hand hygiene sufficient; N95 or special respiratory equipment NOT needed

---

## 4. COMBINED PRECAUTIONS

### When Multiple Transmission-Based Precautions Apply
Some pathogens require BOTH precaution types:
- **Measles:** Airborne + standard precautions (respiratory droplet nuclei)
- **Varicella:** Airborne + standard precautions (can aerosolize from lesion fluid)
- **Certain respiratory viruses:** May require droplet + contact if secretion management issues

---

## HESI High-Yield Rules

### Decision Tree
1. **Is diagnosis suspected but NOT confirmed?** → Implement precautions based on clinical presentation
2. **What is the transmission route?**
   - Respiratory droplet nuclei (remain suspended) → AIRBORNE
   - Large droplets (short range) → DROPLET
   - Skin/environmental surfaces → CONTACT
   - Blood/body fluids → Standard precautions + appropriate PPE

3. **Duration:** Continue until transmission risk resolved (MD order, culture confirmation, symptom improvement per protocol)

### Common HESI Traps
- Assuming droplet precautions sufficient for TB → **WRONG** (TB is airborne)
- Assuming respiratory precautions needed for MRSA → **WRONG** (MRSA is contact)
- Waiting for culture confirmation before implementing precautions → **WRONG** (act on suspicion)
- Confusing negative-pressure room requirement → **Airborne only** (not droplet or contact)
