# Sterile Technique, PPE Donning/Doffing, and Equipment Safety

## Core Concept: Sterile Field Integrity and PPE Sequencing

---

## STERILE FIELD PRINCIPLES

### What IS Part of the Sterile Field
- The contents inside sterile packages (drapes, instruments, supplies)
- Center 1-inch border of sterile drapes
- Sterile gloved hands and arms (to elbow)
- Sterile gown (front and sleeves)

### What IS NOT Part of the Sterile Field (Non-Sterile)
- **Outer wrapper/packaging surfaces** (inherently non-sterile by design)
- Table edges and surfaces beyond the drape border
- Below-waist level items or actions
- Non-sterile gloved hands

### Critical Sterile Field Rule
**Touching the outer wrapper of a sterile package does NOT contaminate the contents.**
- The wrapper's entire purpose is to protect contents while remaining non-sterile
- Expected contact with outer surfaces = not a breach
- Wasting sterile supplies due to overcautious wrapper concerns = poor resource management

### What DOES Contaminate a Sterile Field
- Touching the **sterile contents** with unsterile hands
- **Below-waist level contact** (level cannot be assured as sterile)
- **Unsterile gloved hand contact** with sterile field
- **Coughing, sneezing, or talking over sterile field**
- Items left **open and unattended** in the field
- **Moisture seeping through** drapes (moisture conducts bacteria)
- **Non-sterile item suddenly appearing** in the field

## STERILE DRESSING CHANGE SEQUENCING (e.g., CVC)

### Correct Sequence
1. **Set up sterile field FIRST** (with all needed supplies)
   - Open sterile packages containing drapes, dressings, cleansing solutions
   - Arrange on work surface
2. **Perform hand hygiene** (soap and water or alcohol-based sanitizer)
3. **Don sterile gloves**
4. **Remove old dressing** (gloved hands now sterile, won't contaminate)
5. **Proceed with cleansing and new dressing application**

### Why This Sequence
- Sterile gloves donned AFTER field setup means you don't waste sterile glove time searching for supplies
- Gloves remain pristine for actual sterile procedure
- Old dressing (contaminated) removed AFTER sterile gloves donned = less self-contamination risk
- Efficient workflow

### Why Reverse Order Fails
- If you remove old dressing first (with clean gloves), you expose yourself to contamination
- Then setting up field afterward = longer exposure time, higher contamination risk
- Wastes clean glove protection before sterile gloves donned

---

## PPE DONNING SEQUENCE

### Correct Order (Least to Most Restrictive)
1. **Gown** (covers clothing, body)
2. **Mask** (face protection)
3. **Eye protection/face shield** (if splash risk)
4. **Gloves** (last, covers gown cuff)

### Rationale
- Donning in one order ensures proper coverage and adherence
- Gloves overlay gown cuff, preventing pathogen entry at wrist
- Each item applied over previous for maximum barrier integrity

---

## PPE DOFFING SEQUENCE (MOST IMPORTANT FOR EXAM)

### Correct Order (Most to Least Contaminated)
1. **Gloves FIRST** (most contaminated layer)
2. **Gown SECOND** (outer barrier, now exposed)
3. **Mask THIRD** (face protection no longer frontline)
4. **Eye protection LAST** (least likely to be directly contaminated)

### Why This Sequence Minimizes Self-Contamination
- Remove gloves first while gown still on = contains contaminated glove removal
- Remove gown while bare hands haven't contaminated gown's clean side
- Remove mask over clean field
- Eye protection removed last over cleanest remaining surfaces

### Why Reverse Order Would Fail
- Removing gown before gloves = exposed hands contact contaminated gown's exterior
- Then removing contaminated gloves = self-inoculation risk
- This increases contamination exposure, not reduces it

### Hand Hygiene During Doffing
- Perform hand hygiene AFTER removing gloves (they may be the only barrier between contamination and hands)
- Optional: alcohol-based sanitizer after glove removal can be acceptable if hands not visibly soiled
- Soap and water preferred in high-contamination scenarios

---

## CVC DRESSING CHANGE—INTEGRATION OF ALL PRINCIPLES

### Full Sequence
1. Assess patient, explain procedure
2. Gather supplies, set up sterile field (while wearing clean gloves or no gloves)
3. Perform hand hygiene
4. Don sterile gloves
5. Remove old dressing (now with sterile gloves, contamination minimized)
6. Cleanse site with antiseptic per protocol
7. Apply new dressing
8. Remove sterile gloves
9. Remove gown (if worn)
10. Remove mask
11. Remove eye protection
12. Perform hand hygiene
13. Document

### HESI High-Yield Principle
**Sterile field and dressing changes = sequencing > speed**. Correct order prevents contamination more reliably than rushing.

## HESI TRAP QUESTIONS

| Trap | Incorrect Answer | Correct Answer |
|------|------------------|-----------------|
| Touched sterile wrapper | "Discard all supplies, restart" | "Continue; wrapper is non-sterile by design" |
| How to doff PPE | "Gown → gloves → mask" | "Gloves → gown → mask → eye protection" |
| When to setup sterile field | "After removing old dressing" | "Before removing dressing; don sterile gloves after" |
| CVC dressing breaks sterility | Document and proceed if only outer wrapper touched | Discard ONLY if actual contents or borders touched |
