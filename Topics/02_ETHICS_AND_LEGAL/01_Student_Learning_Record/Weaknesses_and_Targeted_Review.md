# ETHICS AND LEGAL: Weaknesses and Targeted Review

Date: March 20, 2026

## Priority Review Targets

### 1) Informed Consent Precision
Observed pattern:
- Correct big-picture understanding, but occasional mixing of categories when consent is questionable.

Differentiate these clearly:
- Information gap: patient does not understand risks/benefits/alternatives.
- Coercion/voluntariness problem: patient feels forced.
- Incapacity problem: patient cannot understand or reason.

Targeted drill:
- For each scenario, label the issue as one of the 3 categories above and state the nurse escalation step.

### 2) Therapeutic Scripting Under Family Distress
Observed pattern:
- Strong intent (compassion + boundaries) with room to structure language in sequence.

Use sequence:
1. Validate emotion
2. State legal/ethical boundary
3. Reframe to honoring patient wishes
4. Offer specific support actions

Practice prompt:
- Write a 4-sentence response to: "Do everything, she did not mean her DNR."

### 3) Scope and Delegation Legal Boundaries
Status:
- Section queued for next set of ethics questions.

Pre-read checklist:
- RN cannot delegate: initial assessment, teaching, evaluation, unstable judgment tasks.
- UAP can perform: routine, predictable, stable tasks after RN assessment.

## Quick Self-Test
1. Patient signs consent but asks, "Am I getting my kidney removed today?" (wrong procedure understanding)
- Category?
- First RN action?

2. Family insists on CPR despite valid living will.
- Who has priority?
- Best RN communication frame?

3. Competent patient refuses life-saving intervention.
- Which principle governs?
- What must be documented?

## Expected Mastery Standard

- Use a 4-part therapeutic communication script without prompting
- Correctly identify patient-wish precedence in end-of-life conflicts
