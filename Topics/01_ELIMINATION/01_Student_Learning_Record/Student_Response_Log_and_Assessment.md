### Section 4: Drug Mechanism - ACE Inhibitors & NSAIDs

**Question:** Explain the mechanism of action for reducing GFR from ACE inhibitors and NSAIDs.

**Student's Answer:**
> "I have the understanding that ACE inhibitors and NSAIDs cause water retention."

**Assessment:** ❌ **PARTIALLY CORRECT BUT MISFRAMED**

**Educator's Teaching:**
- **Reframe:** They don't cause "water retention"; they **decrease GFR/urine output**
- **ACE Inhibitors mechanism:** Block angiotensin II → lose efferent arteriole vasoconstriction → decreased glomerular pressure → decreased GFR
- **NSAIDs mechanism:** Inhibit prostaglandins → lose afferent arteriole dilation → decreased glomerular blood flow → decreased GFR

**Weakness Category:** Understanding drug mechanisms at the cellular/physiologic level; semantic precision (water retention vs. decreased output)

### Section 7: CAUTI Mechanism & Organisms

**Question:** Why is catheterization such a high-risk procedure for infection? What organisms commonly cause CAUTIs?

**Student's Answer:**
> "Epithelial tissue of the urethra can be harmed by insertion; lack of urine flow afterwards through the urethra to clear pathogenic organisms from colonizing. E. coli is the most common CAUTI organism."

**Assessment:** ⚠ **GOOD BUT INCOMPLETE MECHANISM**

**What Student Got Right:**
- ✅ Identified epithelial trauma
- ✅ Identified loss of normal urine flow as cleansing mechanism
- ✅ Correctly named E. coli as most common

**What Student Missed:**
- ❌ **Biofilm formation** (the most important mechanism explaining persistent CAUTIs)
- ❌ Other common organisms (Pseudomonas, Proteus, Klebsiella, enterococcus)
- ❌ Foreign body concept and its role in infection

**Better Complete Answer:**
1. Epithelial trauma breaches protective barrier
2. Introduction of bacteria during insertion (if not sterile)
3. Loss of normal urine flow to flush organisms
4. **BIOFILM FORMATION** on catheter surface—bacteria form protective matrix that antibiotics can't penetrate
5. Prolonged dwell time allows biofilm to establish

**Weakness Category:** Missing the biofilm concept—a critical concept explaining why CAUTIs are so hard to treat and why removal is best prevention
