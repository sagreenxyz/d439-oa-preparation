# Student Study Guide: OA Readiness Questionnaire

## Overview

This guide groups all 50 exam questions into **strategic study clusters** based on the nursing concepts they share. Studying by cluster lets you build one mental framework and then apply it across multiple questions — which is far more efficient than reviewing questions in random order.

**Quick Stats:**
- 50 questions | 100 points total (2 pts each)
- 38 single-choice questions
- 12 select-all-that-apply (SATA) questions

> **SATA tip:** On every SATA question, treat each option as a true/false statement independently. Do not let the number of correct answers you have already found stop you from evaluating remaining options.

## Study Cluster 1 — Wound Care & Skin Integrity (7 Questions)

**Questions:** Q10, Q12, Q13, Q41, Q43, Q46, Q50

### Core Concepts to Master

| Concept | Key Fact |
|---|---|
| Wound healing by intention | Primary = clean surgical wound closed immediately; Secondary = wound left open to granulate; Tertiary = delayed closure |
| Pressure ulcer staging | Stage I = non-blanchable redness, intact skin; Stage II = partial thickness (blister/shallow crater); Stage III = full-thickness with visible subQ fat, possible slough; Stage IV = bone/tendon/muscle exposed |
| Wound drainage types | Serous = clear/watery; Sanguineous = bloody; Serosanguineous = pink/mixed; **Purulent = thick, yellow/green = infection** |
| Wound infection risk | Puncture wounds = highest risk (deep, narrow, anaerobic environment) |
| Wound healing factors | Protein is the single most important nutritional factor; also: circulation, glucose control, vitamin C, zinc |
| Diabetic neuropathy foot care | Daily foot inspection, cotton socks, supportive footwear; never apply heat or walk barefoot |

### Question-by-Question Highlights

- **Q12**: Stage IV = **full-thickness wound repair** (not partial-thickness, not primary intention)
- **Q13**: Thick yellow drainage = **purulent** (not serous, not sanguineous)
- **Q41 (SATA)**: Stage III features = full-thickness loss with *visible subQ fat* AND possible slough/eschar — not bone/tendon (that is Stage IV)
- **Q43**: Kitchen knife cut = primary intention (clean, closed wound)
- **Q46**: Nail puncture = highest infection risk (depth + contaminated object)
- **Q50**: Prolonged bed rest + no healing → most likely cause is **inadequate protein intake**
- **Q10**: Diabetic neuropathy → daily foot assessment + cotton socks + supportive footwear

## Study Cluster 2 — Infection Control & Asepsis (4 Questions)

**Questions:** Q01, Q03, Q18, Q44

### Core Concepts to Master

| Concept | Key Fact |
|---|---|
| Medical asepsis | Reduces/prevents microorganism spread; hand washing is the cornerstone |
| Surgical (sterile) asepsis | Eliminates all microorganisms; sterile field must stay at/above waist; never turn your back on it |
| CAUTI prevention | Drainage bag always below bladder; clean insertion site; secure catheter to thigh |
| Droplet precautions | Surgical mask within 3 ft; private room or cohort; gloves; client wears mask outside room |
| Sterile field rules | Open packages away from body; reach over only with sterile gloves; outer 1-inch border is contaminated |

### Question-by-Question Highlights

- **Q01 (SATA)**: CAUTI — keep bag below bladder ✓, clean insertion site daily ✓, secure to thigh ✓ — *never* clamp tubing or disconnect frequently
- **Q03**: Medical asepsis — **hand washing** is correct; "below waist" and "pour sterile solution onto a field" are surgical asepsis actions
- **Q18 (SATA)**: Droplet precautions — *surgical* mask (not N95/airborne), private room, gloves, mask for client, hand hygiene
- **Q44 (SATA)**: Sterile field — open away from body, keep at/above waist, sterile gloves before touching, reach *over* (not across/behind) the field

## Study Cluster 3 — Oxygenation & Respiratory Care (6 Questions)

**Questions:** Q02, Q14, Q21, Q22, Q28, Q39

### Core Concepts to Master

| Concept | Key Fact |
|---|---|
| Nasal cannula skin care | Pad tubing with gauze/foam; reassess frequently — do not tighten the cannula |
| Oxygen safety | No smoking signs; remove petroleum-based products; ground electrical equipment; cotton bedding; secure O₂ tanks upright |
| Suctioning | Allow adequate time *between* passes to prevent hypoxia; do not specify a fixed number of passes |
| CPAP | Must be used every night consistently; stopping early because you "feel better" is a common misconception |
| Aspiration prevention (tube feeding) | HOB elevated 30–45 degrees (Semi-Fowler's) during and after feeding |
| Comfort positioning (dyspnea) | Elevate HOB first before medications in a hospice/palliative context |

### Question-by-Question Highlights

- **Q02**: Skin irritation from O₂ cannula → **pad tubing with gauze/foam** (not tighten, not lubricate the nostrils with petroleum products)
- **Q14**: Suctioning → **allow adequate time between passes** (avoid hypoxia)
- **Q21 (SATA)**: O₂ safety — remove petroleum lotions, no smoking signs, ground equipment, cotton bedding, secure tanks upright — patient should *never* self-adjust flow rate
- **Q22**: CPAP misconception — "stop using if you feel better" = **needs further teaching**
- **Q28**: Hospice dyspnea → **elevate HOB first** (non-pharmacological comfort measure)
- **Q39**: Enteral feeding → **Semi-Fowler's (30–45°)** to prevent aspiration

## Study Cluster 4 — Vital Signs & Clinical Assessment (6 Questions)

**Questions:** Q09, Q15, Q17, Q31, Q33, Q36

### Core Concepts to Master

| Concept | Key Fact |
|---|---|
| Pain assessment (first action) | Always **assess** before treating — characteristics (PQRST) before calling provider or giving medication |
| PQRST | P = Provocation/Palliation; Q = Quality; R = Region/Radiation; **S = Severity (0–10 scale)**; T = Timing |
| Subjective vs. objective data | Subjective = what the patient *says* (feelings, fears, descriptions); Objective = what the nurse *observes/measures* |
| Tympanic temperature technique | Adults: pull pinna **up and back**; Children under 3: pull pinna **down and back** (Q15 is a 4-year-old → **up and back**) |
| Temperature method selection | Dehydrated/vomiting patient → **axillary** (not oral due to vomiting risk) |
| Blood pressure accuracy | Avoid nicotine/caffeine within 30 minutes; proper cuff size; arm at heart level; patient rested 5 minutes |

### Question-by-Question Highlights

- **Q09**: Post-op pain → **assess characteristics first** (not give medication, not call provider)
- **Q15**: 4-year-old tympanic temp → **pull pinna up and back** (4 years old ≥ 3 years)
- **Q17 (SATA)**: Subjective = patient *reporting* fear or excitement; Objective = nurse-measured temp, observed wound, observed pacing
- **Q31**: "S" in PQRST = **severity** (0–10 scale)
- **Q33**: Dehydration + vomiting → **axillary** temperature (avoids oral route issues)
- **Q36**: BP accuracy → **avoid caffeine/nicotine within 30 min** before measurement

## Study Cluster 5 — Mobility, Exercise & Assistive Devices (4 Questions)

**Questions:** Q16, Q19, Q24, Q34

### Core Concepts to Master

| Concept | Key Fact |
|---|---|
| Cane use | Hold in the **strong hand**; move cane and weak leg forward together; then strong leg |
| Crutch fitting | 2–3 finger widths between axilla and crutch pad; elbow bent ~30 degrees at hand grip; weight on **hands**, not axillae |
| ROM exercises | Stop if patient reports fatigue or pain; reassess the care plan; assess for other symptoms |
| Exercise types | Isotonic = **movement** (walking, swimming, cycling, jogging, dancing); Isometric = muscle contraction without movement; Resistive isometric = pushing against resistance |

### Question-by-Question Highlights

- **Q16**: Cane with hemiparesis → **strong hand** holds cane (do not lean on cane, not 12 inches below waist)
- **Q19 (SATA)**: Correct crutch fit: elbow bent ~30° ✓, 2–3 finger widths at axilla ✓ — hand grips are NOT at mid-forearm; weight is on hands NOT axillae
- **Q24 (SATA)**: Patient says "too tired" → **stop**, **reassess plan**, **assess for other symptoms** — do not encourage to push through
- **Q34**: Isotonic exercise involves actual **movement**: bicycling, swimming, walking, jogging, dancing

## Study Cluster 6 — Nutrition, Elimination & Specimen Collection (5 Questions)

**Questions:** Q20, Q27, Q29, Q45, Q33*

*Q33 also appears in Cluster 4 (vital signs context); study the specimen-collection aspect here.

### Core Concepts to Master

| Concept | Key Fact |
|---|---|
| Steatorrhea | Fat malabsorption → stool is **frothy and foul-smelling** |
| Clear liquid diet | Includes: broth, coffee, tea, gelatin, popsicles, clear juices — Excludes: ice cream, dairy, vegetable juice, anything opaque |
| 24-hour urine | Discard **first** morning void; collect all urine for 24 hours; refrigerate/keep on ice; void at end of 24 hours and ADD to collection; notify lab if any is discarded |
| Urine specimen from catheter | Collect from the **port** (sampling port), not the drainage bag; use ~3 mL for culture |
| Catheter specimen error | Never collect from the drainage bag collection receptacle (it is not sterile) |

### Question-by-Question Highlights

- **Q20**: Steatorrhea = **frothy and foul-smelling** (fat in stool)
- **Q27 (SATA)**: Clear liquids: broth ✓, coffee ✓, gelatin ✓, popsicle ✓ — ice cream and vegetable juice are NOT clear liquids
- **Q29 (SATA)**: Urine collection: port aspiration for culture ✓, discard first void on 24-hr collection ✓ — do not collect from drainage receptacle; room-temperature storage is incorrect
- **Q45 (SATA)**: 24-hr urine: discard first morning void ✓, refrigerate ✓, add final void ✓, notify lab if discarded ✓

## Study Cluster 7 — Medication Administration (3 Questions)

**Questions:** Q06, Q25, Q42

### Core Concepts to Master

| Concept | Key Fact |
|---|---|
| Medication safety — patient concern | Always **verify the original prescription** before proceeding |
| Heparin injection | Inject into fatty tissue of abdomen; **at least 2 inches from umbilicus** (not 1 inch); rotate sites; do NOT massage after injection |
| Ear drops — adult | Pull pinna **up and back**; keep affected ear up for 2–3 min after; drops should be room temperature (not refrigerator-cold) |

### Question-by-Question Highlights

- **Q06**: Confused patient questions medication appearance → **check original prescription first** (do not explain, do not encourage them to take it)
- **Q25**: Heparin injection error: **1 inch from umbilicus is wrong** — must be at least 2 inches away
- **Q42**: Adult ear drops → **side-lying with affected ear up** for 2–3 minutes after instillation (also pull pinna up and back for adults)

## Study Cluster 8 — Patient Safety, Legal & Ethical Issues (6 Questions)

**Questions:** Q05, Q23, Q26, Q38, Q48, Q49

### Core Concepts to Master

| Concept | Key Fact |
|---|---|
| Restraints | Two-finger slack between restraint and skin; tie to bed frame (not side rail); least restrictive option; reassess frequently |
| Good Samaritan Law | Protects healthcare providers who give emergency care in good faith off duty — does not protect negligent acts |
| EHR downtime | Use **downtime paper forms**; enter into EHR when system is restored |
| Emancipated minor & HIPAA | Emancipated minors have full adult rights → **no parental notification required** |
| Legal terms | Assault = verbal threat of harm; Battery = unwanted physical contact; Slander = spoken defamation; Libel = written defamation |
| Witness signature | Witness signature only confirms the person **signed the document** in the witness's presence — not that they are informed or competent |

### Question-by-Question Highlights

- **Q05**: Proper restraint → **two fingers fit between restraint and skin** (not supine for wrist, not tied to side rail)
- **Q23**: Good Samaritan → **performing CPR off duty** is protected (transporting in personal vehicle is not)
- **Q26**: EHR outage → **use downtime paper forms** (do not wait; do not rely on memory)
- **Q38**: Emancipated minor → **maintain confidentiality without parental notification**
- **Q48**: "I won't answer your bell" = **assault** (verbal threat that creates fear of harm)
- **Q49**: Witness signature = only confirms the client **physically signed the form** in the witness's presence

## Study Cluster 9 — Prioritization, Delegation & Communication (4 Questions)

**Questions:** Q08, Q30, Q32, Q47

### Core Concepts to Master

| Concept | Key Fact |
|---|---|
| Maslow's hierarchy | Physiological needs first → Safety → Love/Belonging → Esteem → Self-Actualization; breathing/circulation always first |
| Maslow's purpose in nursing | Used to **establish priorities of care** (not for diagnosis, not for communication) |
| UAP scope of practice | When a UAP is about to perform an out-of-scope task → **stop them immediately**, explain, redirect |
| SBAR | S = Situation; B = Background; A = Assessment; **R = Recommendation** (nurse's proposed action/request) |

### Question-by-Question Highlights

- **Q08**: Maslow priority → client with **shortness of breath** (physiological/airway need) over sadness, bowel movement, or emotional concern
- **Q30**: UAP out-of-scope → **stop, explain, redirect** (do not allow, do not report without intervening first)
- **Q32**: Maslow's hierarchy is used for **establishing priorities of care**
- **Q47**: SBAR "Recommendation" → nurse's *recommendation/request* for action: "I believe the client needs immediate intervention"

## Study Cluster 10 — Patient Education & Therapeutic Communication (3 Questions)

**Questions:** Q04, Q07, Q40

### Core Concepts to Master

| Concept | Key Fact |
|---|---|
| Patient positioning rationale | Semi-Fowler's position (30–45°) improves respiratory expansion — most important reason to state |
| Language barrier & teach-back | Use a **professional interpreter**; have the patient demonstrate/explain back; do not rely on gestures or family members |
| Therapeutic relationship phases | Orientation phase = **building rapport**; Working phase = identifying/addressing problems; Termination phase = closure |

### Question-by-Question Highlights

- **Q04**: Semi-Fowler's rationale → "raising the head supports **your breathing**" (not drainage, not elimination, not gas)
- **Q07**: Non-English speaker + wound care teaching → **professional interpreter + teach-back** (do not use English-only written instructions)
- **Q40**: First meeting = orientation phase → priority action is **building rapport**

## Study Cluster 11 — Comfort, Psychosocial & End-of-Life Care (4 Questions)

**Questions:** Q11, Q35, Q37, Q28*

*Q28 also appears in Cluster 3 (Oxygenation); study the psychosocial/comfort aspect here.

### Core Concepts to Master

| Concept | Key Fact |
|---|---|
| Restorative sleep | Back massage, dim lights, reduce noise, manage pain, avoid late diuretics — do not wake frequently or encourage TV |
| Therapeutic response to grief | Use empathy and open acknowledgment; avoid minimizing, platitudes ("she's in a better place"), or false reassurance |
| Signs of impending death | Cold, clammy, wax-like skin; mottling; Cheyne-Stokes respirations; decreased urine output; these indicate death is near |
| Hospice comfort (dyspnea) | Non-pharmacological first (positioning), then pharmacological if needed |

### Question-by-Question Highlights

- **Q11 (SATA)**: Sleep promotion: back massage ✓, reduce noise/dim lights ✓, avoid late diuretics ✓, manage pain ✓ — do NOT encourage TV or wake every 2 hours
- **Q35**: Grieving mother → **"It must be hard to deal with such a precious loss"** (empathetic acknowledgment — not minimizing or platitudes)
- **Q37**: Cold, clammy, wax-like skin in terminally ill patient → patient is **likely to die soon**

## SATA Strategy Quick Reference

SATA questions appear in: Q01, Q11, Q17, Q18, Q19, Q21, Q24, Q27, Q29, Q41, Q44, Q45

**Step-by-step approach:**
1. Read the stem carefully and identify what is being asked.
2. Evaluate **each option independently** — true or false?
3. Do not assume a minimum or maximum number of correct answers.
4. Watch for absolute words ("always," "never," "all") — these often signal incorrect options.
5. Watch for options that are partially correct — the *entire* option must be correct to select it.

## Common Exam Traps on This Question Set

| Trap | Example | Remember |
|---|---|---|
| Picking the "do something" option before assessing | Q09: giving pain meds before assessing | **Assess first, always** |
| Confusing adult vs. child ear/temp technique | Q15, Q42: tympanic temp, ear drops | Adults: up and back; children < 3: down and back |
| Confusing medical vs. surgical asepsis rules | Q03, Q44: "below waist" = surgical, not medical | Hand washing = medical asepsis |
| Misidentifying the SBAR component | Q47: "I believe…needs intervention" = Recommendation | S=Situation, B=Background, A=Assessment, R=Recommendation |
| Misidentifying subjective vs. objective data | Q17: pacing = objective (observed); fear = subjective (reported) | Subjective = stated by patient |
| Umbilicus distance for Heparin | Q25: 1 inch = wrong (needs 2 inches) | **At least 2 inches** from umbilicus |
| Stage III vs. Stage IV pressure ulcer | Q41: fat visible = III; bone/tendon exposed = IV | Depth determines stage |
| CPAP misconception | Q22: stopping when feeling better = wrong | Use every night, indefinitely |
