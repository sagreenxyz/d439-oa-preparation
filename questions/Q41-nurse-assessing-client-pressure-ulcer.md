# Question 41

A nurse is assessing a client’s pressure ulcer on the sacrum. Which of the following findings are consistent with a Stage III pressure ulcer? Select All That Apply

**Type:** Multiple choice

**Points:** 2 Points

## Options

- CORRECT ==> Full-thickness tissue loss with visible subcutaneous fat but no bone, tendon, or muscle exposure \[Stage III\]
- Intact skin with non-blanchable redness over a bony prominence \[Stage I\]
- Partial-thickness loss of dermis presenting as a shallow open ulcer with a red-pink wound bed \[Stage II\]
- CORRECT ==> Presence of slough or eschar that may obscure the wound bed \[Stage III\]
- Full-thickness tissue loss exposing bone, tendon, or muscle \[Stage IV\]
- Blister or shallow crater involving the epidermis and dermis \[Stage II\]
